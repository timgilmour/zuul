# Vocabulary — Environments

Not backgrounds — renders are always isolated. These are the *origin environments* a subject comes from. They inform weathering, wear patterns, material degradation, colour palette, and design logic without appearing in the image.

## Fantasy

### Underdark
*Design implications:* no sunlight → pale or bioluminescent skin tones, fungal motifs, adapted eyes (glowing, large), armour repaired with cave materials, everything coated in fine mineral dust
*Weathering language:* `mineral-dusted`, `cave-worn`, `phosphorescent accents`, `repaired with bone or chitin`

### Surface Wilderness
*Design implications:* weather exposure, mud, plant stains, sun-bleached fabrics, pragmatic repairs
*Weathering language:* `mud-caked boots`, `sun-faded cloth`, `field-repaired stitching`, `bark-stained leather`

### Royal Court / Urban
*Design implications:* maintained equipment, decorative elements, status signalling, less battlefield wear
*Weathering language:* `well-maintained`, `ceremonially polished`, `sigil-engraved`, `tailored`

### Enchanted Wilderness (Fairytale / High Fantasy)
*Design implications:* magical aura exposure — organic surfaces faintly luminous, flora motifs integrated into design, pristine despite wildness
*Weathering language:* `touched by enchantment`, `living-vine detail`, `moss-grown stone`, `faintly luminous at edges`

### Wuxia Mountain (Wuxia)
*Design implications:* high-altitude wind exposure, monastery rigour, silk worn under armour, minimal ornament except earned rank marks
*Weathering language:* `wind-worn silk`, `altitude-faded fabric`, `ink-stained sleeve edge`, `pine-resin implied`

## Horror

### Ancestral Manor / Crypt (Gothic Horror)
*Design implications:* damp stone interior, generations of neglect, candlewax and old blood stains, fabric rotting at edges but cold-preserved
*Weathering language:* `damp-stone mildew`, `candlewax drips`, `silk rotting at hem`, `tarnished and unpolished`, `cold-preserved`

### Suburban Interior (Movie Horror)
*Design implications:* ordinary domestic wear — a normal person in a nightmare; clothes from everyday life, blood as disruption of the mundane
*Weathering language:* `everyday-worn`, `bloodstained disruption`, `torn by panic`, `suburban-mundane`, `fluorescent-lit pallor`

### Rural / Pagan Ground (Folk Horror)
*Design implications:* outdoor rural isolation, ritual use of natural materials (woad, ash, harvest plants), archaic craft, earth and blood staining
*Weathering language:* `woad-marked`, `ash-smeared`, `field-worn`, `hand-stitched rough cloth`, `harvest-stained`

### Eldritch Ruin / Void (Cosmic Horror)
*Design implications:* exposure to wrongness — subtle biological alteration, materials that shouldn't exist, geometry that doesn't resolve, cold that isn't temperature
*Weathering language:* `reality-adjacent wrongness`, `void-touched`, `biological misalignment`, `geometry slightly off`

### Laboratory / Surgical (Body Horror)
*Design implications:* clinical sterility violated — surgical instruments, specimen fluid staining, latex and steel, organic matter in wrong contexts
*Weathering language:* `clinical-sterile base`, `organic intrusion`, `specimen-fluid stained`, `sutured joins`, `latex-handling marks`

## Sci-Fi

### Deep Space
*Design implications:* vacuum patches, radiation scoring, micrometeor impacts, zero-g tether points
*Weathering language:* `vacuum-patched`, `radiation-scored hull`, `zero-g anchor points`, `EVA wear`

### Industrial / Forge World
*Design implications:* heat scoring, chemical stains, heavy-use wear, improvised reinforcement
*Weathering language:* `heat-scored`, `chemical-stained`, `weld-patched`, `soot-blackened`

### Corporate / Urban
*Design implications:* clean but functional, brand insignia, maintained but impersonal
*Weathering language:* `corporate-issued`, `regulation-standard`, `lightly worn`, `ID-tagged`

### Cyberpunk Street (Cyberpunk)
*Design implications:* neon-lit rain-soaked urban density, street-level grime, black-market modifications, vendor-stall repairs
*Weathering language:* `rain-soaked`, `neon-reflective residue`, `black-market patched`, `street-worn`, `corporate logo defaced`

### Post-Human Lab (Post-Human)
*Design implications:* synthetic materials interfacing with organic, clean-room exposure, bioluminescent fluid traces, interface ports and seams
*Weathering language:* `interface-port scarring`, `synthetic-organic seam`, `bioluminescent trace fluid`, `clean-room baseline`

## Modern

### Combat Zone / Field (Military, WWII)
*Design implications:* active battlefield wear — mud, blast scoring, improvised repairs, unit markings stencilled or painted over in the field
*Weathering language:* `mud-encrusted`, `blast-scored`, `field-patched`, `stencilled unit marking`, `sweat-stained webbing`

### WWI Trench (WWI)
*Design implications:* persistent wet mud, gas-mask rubber perished at edges, brass verdigris, fabric rotted at hem from standing water, rifle-oil darkening
*Weathering language:* `trench-mud soaked`, `gas-rubber perished`, `water-rotted hem`, `rifle-oil dark`, `shell-scar implied`

### Rain-Slick City (Noir, Espionage)
*Design implications:* urban rain exposure, cigarette smoke absorbed into fabric, shoe leather on wet pavement, concealed-carry wear at holster points
*Weathering language:* `rain-dampened wool`, `smoke-absorbed fabric`, `holster-worn leather`, `pavement-splashed hem`

### Urban Rooftop / Cityscape (Superhero)
*Design implications:* high-altitude wind, rooftop grit, costume wear from repeated impact, cape edge fraying from debris and rooftop edges
*Weathering language:* `rooftop-grit abraded`, `wind-stressed fabric`, `impact-worn at knees and elbows`, `cape edge frayed`

## Science-Fantasy

### Victorian Industrial City (Steampunk)
*Design implications:* coal soot settling on all surfaces, grease at mechanical joints, brass polish residue on decorative elements, wool damp from fog
*Weathering language:* `coal-soot patina`, `grease-stained at joint`, `brass-polish residue`, `fog-damp wool`, `clockwork oil seep`

### Interwar Industrial (Dieselpunk)
*Design implications:* factory floor oil and grime, industrial paint chips at rivet heads, art-deco ornament dulled by factory exposure
*Weathering language:* `factory-floor oil`, `paint-chipped rivet head`, `arc-weld scar`, `deco-gold tarnished`, `industrial grime baseline`

### Atomic-Age Suburb / Lab (Atompunk)
*Design implications:* domestic clean surface with era-appropriate anxiety beneath — aluminium gleam, pastel fabric, radiation-badge as standard accessory
*Weathering language:* `aluminium-gleam surface`, `pastel-fresh fabric`, `radiation-badge clipped on`, `domestic-clean with lab accent`

### Irradiated Wasteland (Gamma World)
*Design implications:* scavenged and jury-rigged from multiple original sources, radiation burns on organic surfaces, every piece a different provenance
*Weathering language:* `radiation-bleached`, `salvage-assembled`, `jury-rigged repair`, `pre-war logo barely legible`, `mutation-scarred organic`

### Alien World (Sword-and-Planet)
*Design implications:* alien soil tones (red sand, purple dust), exotic flora stains, twin-sun UV bleaching, alien material substitutions in repairs
*Weathering language:* `alien-soil stained`, `twin-sun UV bleached`, `exotic flora residue`, `alien-material patched repair`

## Historical

### Mediterranean / Near East (Ancient)
*Design implications:* harsh sun bleaching, dust ingress into every joint, heat expansion damage, bronze verdigris, oil and resin preservation
*Weathering language:* `sun-bleached limestone dust`, `bronze verdigris`, `heat-expanded joins`, `resin-sealed`, `sand-abraded edges`

### Northern Sea / Fjord (Viking)
*Design implications:* salt spray corrosion, grey Northern light, animal fat preservation on leather, fur compressed from long wear, rune carvings on personal gear
*Weathering language:* `salt-spray corroded`, `fat-preserved leather`, `fur-compressed`, `rune-carved personal mark`, `grey-sky patina`

### Feudal / Castle (Medieval)
*Design implications:* mud and stable staining, hearth smoke absorbed into fabric, heraldic colour worn at folds, ecclesiastical polish on ceremonial pieces
*Weathering language:* `stable-mud baseline`, `hearth-smoke absorbed`, `heraldic colour faded at fold`, `ecclesiastically polished where ceremonial`

### Feudal Japan (Samurai)
*Design implications:* lacquer cracked at articulation points, silk worn at collar edge, clan mon polished, bamboo-stain on outdoor equipment
*Weathering language:* `lacquer-cracked at joint`, `silk-worn collar edge`, `mon polished`, `bamboo-stained`, `dojo-floor worn`

### Open Ocean / Colonial Port (Age of Sail)
*Design implications:* salt spray and sun on all surfaces, tar from rigging work, brass fittings verdigris, rope burn on hands and gloves, waterline staining
*Weathering language:* `salt-weathered`, `tar-stained from rigging`, `brass verdigris fittings`, `waterline stained`, `sun-bleached sailcloth`

## Western

### Open Frontier Range (Classic Western)
*Design implications:* sun bleaching on all fabrics, trail dust in seams, saddle-leather sweat staining, horse-hair transfer, long-distance road wear
*Weathering language:* `sun-bleached trail-worn`, `saddle-sweat stained`, `trail-dust in every seam`, `horse-hair transfer`, `boot heel worn to one side`

### Desert Canyon / Ghost Town (Spaghetti Western)
*Design implications:* extreme sun bleaching to bone white, sweat rings on hat band, blued gun-metal worn to grey, poncho shoulder-worn through
*Weathering language:* `sun-bleached bone`, `sweat-ring hat band`, `blued-steel worn grey`, `poncho shoulder-worn`, `grit in every crease`

### Cursed Frontier (Weird West)
*Design implications:* normal frontier wear with corruption intruding — dark staining where the supernatural has touched, silver polish on protective items, burnt-sage residue
*Weathering language:* `frontier-worn baseline`, `corruption-stained intrusion`, `silver-polished protection`, `burnt-sage residue`, `wrong shadow implied`

### Post-Collapse Frontier (Apocalypse West)
*Design implications:* pre-war gear repurposed, salvage from multiple eras, bleached by harsh unfiltered sun, everything repaired until repair is the design
*Weathering language:* `pre-war salvage`, `bleached by unfiltered sun`, `multi-era repair layers`, `wire-and-tape join`, `faded brand logo barely legible`
